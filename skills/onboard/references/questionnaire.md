# Onboarding questionnaire

Ask these in small, related batches. Skip anything already known. Everything can be "TBD — confirm" and revisited. Prefer labels or last-4 over full sensitive numbers.

## A. Student & school

1. Student's preferred name (and legal name if different — used only on official forms).
2. School / college, and program or major (or "undecided").
3. Start term and expected graduation (e.g., Fall 2026 → Spring 2030).
4. **Term system:** semester or quarter? Roughly how many terms per year are funded?
5. Enrollment status target (full-time / part-time) and typical credit load per term.
6. Student ID or portal login handle, if they want it on file (optional).

## B. Costs

7. Do they have an official Cost of Attendance or a current bill yet? (If yes, offer to ingest it instead of asking for numbers.)
8. Housing plan: on-campus, off-campus, or commuting? Meal plan?
9. Any known cost buckets to track separately (transportation, a parking permit, lab/course fees, a car)?

## C. Funding sources (the important part)

For each source the student has or expects, capture the fields in `funding-sources.md`. Walk the checklist:

10. **Grants** — Pell, state grant, institutional grant? (need-based; usually renew via annual application)
11. **Scholarships** — merit, departmental, external/private? Renewal conditions (GPA, credits)?
12. **Savings plans** — 529, ESA, or ordinary savings? Approximate balance and who controls it?
13. **Veteran / military benefits** — GI Bill (which chapter), dependents' assistance, a state veteran tuition waiver? (These have finite months, certification steps, and sometimes delimiting dates — capture carefully.)
14. **Loans** — federal (subsidized/unsubsidized), PLUS, private? Amounts and terms if known.
15. **Employment** — work-study award, student job income?
16. **Family / other contributions** — see the supporter section below.
17. Is there a **preferred order** to spend these (e.g., "use the waiver first, preserve the benefit, tap savings last")? Capture it as the allocation priority.

## D. Supporters / contributors (zero or more — no assumptions)

For each person or entity contributing money or managing the process:

18. Role label (parent, guardian, grandparent, sponsor, the student themselves, other).
19. What have they committed to cover (e.g., "room & board," "a fixed $X/term," "gap after aid")?
20. How is it paid (directly to the school, reimbursement, a shared account)?
21. Cadence and any validation step (e.g., "confirm each spring before housing deadlines").

Do not assume there are two supporters, or any particular relationship between them. One is fine. Zero is fine (student-funded).

## E. Access & accounts

22. Should a supporter have access to the student's records/billing? (Many schools require the student to grant this explicitly — note it as a to-do if so.)
23. Where should reminders go — which `~~calendar`, if any? Who should receive them?

## F. Storage & preferences

24. Where should the knowledge base live? (Local Markdown files by default, or a `~~knowledge base` connector.)
25. Output style preferences (tone, brevity, tables vs. prose).
26. Anything else they want tracked (internships, study abroad, a post-graduation plan)?

## Closing

Summarize what you captured, list the TBDs as data gaps, and confirm before seeding the knowledge base.
